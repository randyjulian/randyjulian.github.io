Fraud Analytics: Identity Theft

Behalf

Filtering Identity Theft

I spearheaded an identity theft model under the supervision of a senior data scientist. I talked to various stakeholders: the risk analysts, customer success team and my CTO to learn how to identify the behavior and traits of identity thefts and to implement their manual flagging methods as variables in SQL. Then, I filtered and extracted data using SQL and used the existing graph theory research to identify the networks. I built many iterations of logistic regression and random forest models to successfully identify 800 cases of identity theft and achieved a 76% accuracy rate. Through this project, I learned to work independently and convert a business problem to a data science problem.

IOT Device Whitelisting

Data Hackathon for Social Good

IOT Device Whitelisting

I have always been interested in combining my skills in data and my interest in social causes. The opportunity present itself while I was in Tel Aviv and I participated in Data Hackathon for Social Good. My task was to classify unknown IoT device to create a whitelist of devices to ensure security of the server. It was a challenging hackathon as we are up against post-graduates who have much more experience than us but it was a humbling experience to learn from them. In a bid to ensure that we at least had some results that can be submitted within the final few hours, I decided to forego complicated algorithms and instead suggested a very simple but effective method to add the results of models together. Our inexperience played to our advantage and to our pleasant surprise, the model did very well and we clinched the 3rd prize overall.
